A DAY IN THE SAHARA
MOROCCO | BY MAYA RAO | JANUARY 2018
SUNSET IN THE SAHARA
Seeing a sunset in the Sahara is definitely an incredible experience. Watching the sun sink over the dunes, with nothing but sand around you for as far as the eye can see definitely makes you feel like the only person in the world. It's a centering experience, and one I think everyone should have.
10 AM /
Woke up feeling super excited — today, I'm going to the Sahara! Where I'm staying in the Morrocan Sahara is a really far drive from Marrakech, where my family and I are currently, so I'm mentally preparing myself for a really long car ride.
12 PM /
We stop for lunch at a hotel on the way. Unfortunately, the vegetarian options we've found in the more rural parts of Morocco aren't that great, but we wolf down our sad, soggy spaghetti with the reminder that this will all be worth it very soon.
3 PM /
We finally make it to our hotel! Honestly, I don't know what I was expecting, but this hotel is amazing. All the walls are made out of stone, so it's really cool inside, which is so nice after being out in the heat. They have some really cool art on the walls too, and my mom and I spend an hour discussing our favorite pieces.
5 PM /
Time to drive out to the dunes for sunset! The Sahara dunes are about a 30-40 minute drive from our hotel, and we want to get there early so we can pick the "best dune" on which to watch the sunset.
6 PM /
The sunset happened, and it was beautiful (scroll down for a picture!). The sight of seeing the sun set over the sand dunes — it really made me feel like I was in the middle of nowhere, like an explorer. Definitely a top 10 travel moment for me.
8 PM /
After driving back to the hotel, we stop by the restaurant for dinner, which, as usual, has some extremely unappetizing spaghetti. There's a crêpe station, though, so we load up on them before making our way back to our room, tuckered out after the long day of driving. After taking some long showers to get all the sand out of our hair, we collapse into bed.
