JUST TOUCHED DOWN IN LONDON TOWN
No city is as close to my heart as London. I've had some of the best times of my life in this city, but perhaps more importantly, it is here that I discovered who I am, truly. The former seat of empire certainly has an intimidating quality, but Londoners are on average some of the friendliest people I've encountered, though not without a smattering of classic British pessimism. If you've just moved to London, pop down to your neighborhood pub to meet your new neighbors. If you're in London for a quick vacation (though no amount of time will ever be enough to do London justice), I hope this short guide below proves helpful as you plan your trip.
WHAT TO EAT
Split small plates at Dishoom
London is not London without Indian food, and no Indian spot is as storied as Dishoom, with its unparalleled breakfast, lunch and dinner. Pair dinner with one of their many excellent cocktails, and breakfast with bottomless chai. With an expansive menu and limitless drink options, Dishoom can please any palate.
Green Shakshuka and Feta Cheesecake at Honey & Co.
The feta cheesecake at any of Honey & Co.'s restaurants is the stuff of dreams, but every menu item is perfection. Founded by former Ottolenghi head chefs, Honey & Co. is almost unparalleled in its mastery of modern Mediterranean cuisine. Perhaps one of the more unexpected spots for breakfast, Honey & Co. simply cannot be beat.
Spicy Sesame Paste Noodles at Dumpling Shack
This tiny food stand in East London's Spitalfields Market is a must-stop on any food tour of London. With fresh-made dumplings and noodles, Dumpling Shack is easily one of the best lunches (or midday snack stops!) in the city — and it'll knock you back only £10.
WHAT TO DRINK
Alpine Fizz the Library Bar at The Ned
The Ned's Library Bar is the epitome of Old-World London. Tucked in a corner of The Ned's expansive lobby, Library Bar is cozy and small, perfect for intimate conversations. Order from the excellent cocktail menu or take advantage of Library Bar's bespoke table service and create your own martini, mixed in front of you.
Ginger & Kumquat Julep at The City Bar at Sky Garden
Eschew the overdone The Shard for Sky Garden instead. Offering impressive views of the London cityscape from the city's highest aerial garden, City Bar's vibe cannot be beat. Sip one (or three!) of City Bar's botanical-themed cocktails while watching the sun sink over the Thames.
Paloma Gimlet at The Blind Pig
The Blind Pig, a Social Eating House project, routinely tops the list of London's best cocktail bars — not an easy feat. Start off your night with pre-theater drinks at The Blind Pig, head upstairs for a Michelin-starred dinner at Social Eating House, followed by a West End Musical — then come back for a post-theater nightcap.
WHERE TO STAY
Hyde Park: Mandarin Oriental
Mandarin Oriental's Hyde Park hotel has seen more than its fair share of esteemed guests. Former Prime Minister Winston Churchill used to check in to the hotel often, and both Princess Margaret and Queen Elizabeth II took dance lessons here. The Mandarin Oriental is unquestionably a London institution, opulent but fresh and light at the same time.
Bloomsbury: The Bloomsbury Hotel
Steps from the British Museum, the Bloomsbury Hotel proves that you don't have to sacrifice luxury for convenience. Although Bloomsbury has a reputation as a "college town," as the neighborhood is home to some of London's many universities, the Bloomsbury Hotel is most certainly not a college institution — it's luxury in the beating heart of the city.
Belgravia: The Goring
The Goring Hotel, a century-old family-owned hotel in one of West London's poshest neighborhoods, is the epitome of British luxury — elegant, intimate and with exceptional service. Join the crème de la crème of London high society as they pop by for a spot of tea or a drink in the Goring's gold-trimmed bar.

THE ULTIMATE LONDON BUCKET LIST
  1. Spend a whole day wandering through the exhibits at the British Museum.
  2. Catch a West End musical, or, if you're in London in the summer, see a Shakespeare play at The Globe.
  3. Picnic on Primrose Hill.
  4. Go bar-hopping in Shoreditch.
  5. Get lost in the splendor of Hampton Court Palace.
  6. Take this walking tour.
  7. Pop by Claridge's for a full-service, classically British afternoon tea.
  8. Grab lunch (and a Bread Ahead doughnut) at Borough Market.
  9. Shop for antiques at Portobello Road Market.
  10. Visit the Tate Modern.
